Chapter Xv

Intellectual Education

"... To lead the child from the education of the senses to ideas."

Edward Séguin.

The sense exercises constitute a species of auto-education, which, if these exercises be many times repeated, leads to a perfecting of the child's psychosensory processes. The directress must intervene to lead the child from sensations to ideas—from the concrete to the abstract, and to the association of ideas.
